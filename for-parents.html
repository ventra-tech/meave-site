Is this you?

You're a good parent. You're also exhausted.

Most of the families I work with aren't failing — they're overwhelmed. They're doing everything they can, yet still feel like family life has become harder than it should be. Sound familiar?

  • Mornings and bedtimes have turned into battles you brace yourself for every day.
  • You're shouting more than you want to, and carrying the guilt afterwards.
  • Sibling arguments are constant, and nothing seems to work for long.
  • You and your partner disagree about boundaries, routines, or consequences — and it's creating tension at home.
  • Your child is anxious, withdrawn, angry, or struggling at school — and you're not sure what support would genuinely help.
  • You've read the books, listened to the podcasts, followed the parenting accounts… but real life still feels stuck.

If that sounds familiar, you're not alone — and you don't need to figure it out on your own either.

What Parent Support Actually Looks Like

A practical, private plan built around your family.

Together we'll look at what's happening now — not a generic version of it — and build a short, workable plan you can actually use this week. I draw on the Triple P (Positive Parenting Program) framework, adapted to your child's age, temperament and the realities of your household.

  • Confidential, non-judgemental sessions — online or in person
  • Clear strategies for the behaviour you're stuck on
  • Homework that takes minutes, not evenings
  • Monitoring methods to assess your progress
  • Follow-up between sessions when you need it
  • Support for both parents, or co-parents working apart
  • Pre and post evaluation tools provided

How it works

From first call to steady ground — in three clear steps.

1

Discovery call

A free, no-pressure 20-minute conversation. You tell me what's going on and we decide together whether coaching with me is the right fit for you.

2

Shared plan

Once agreed, you'll complete a pre-evaluation form before we begin. We'll also confirm your learning goals together so the support is built around what matters to your family.

3

Steady practice

We confirm the session dates, the workbook is posted to you, and the online link sent. For course attendees, weekly homework brings the strategies to life. Once complete, a post-evaluation is done and I follow up with a 20-minute call the week after.

FAQs

Questions parents ask before booking.

Is this therapy?

No. Parent support is practical, forward-looking, and focused on parenting strategy — not processing trauma or diagnosing mental health conditions. If you or your child need clinical support, I'll tell you honestly and can help you find the right route.

What ages do you work with?

I'm trained in Triple P for families with children from 2 to 16. There are separate and distinct strategies for parents whose children attend primary school and those with teenagers attending secondary school.

Do both parents need to attend?

It helps, but it is not required. I work with single parents, separated parents, co-parents, grandparents, foster parents, and carers.

My child is neurodivergent — can you help?

Yes. A significant portion of the families I work with have children who are either diagnosed or being assessed for autism and ADHD. My approach adjusts accordingly — same evidence base, different tools.

How long does change take?

Parenting support isn't about instant transformations. Most families begin to notice positive shifts within the first few weeks, with stronger and more sustainable changes building over time as new approaches become part of everyday family life.

What if we can't afford the full programme?

I hold a small number of reduced-fee places each month for families who need them. We can also arrange a payment plan to assist some families if required.

Let's have an honest first conversation.

A free 20-minute discovery call — no sales pitch, no pressure. I'll tell you whether I can help, and if I can't, I'll point you to someone who can.
