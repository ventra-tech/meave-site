Parenting in today's world comes with challenges many families never imagined they would face. From the lasting impact of the pandemic to the pressures of social media, key school transitions and a growing number of children with neurodiverse needs, the job has shifted — and parents are doing it without much of a map.

My approach is practical, evidence-based and non-judgemental, helping you build confidence and create calmer, more connected family relationships.

Explore support for parents

Triple P (Positive Parenting Program) is an internationally recognised, evidence-based parenting programme that helps parents build positive, confident relationships with their children. Rather than offering a one-size-fits-all approach, Triple P provides practical tools and strategies that can be tailored to each family's needs.

I have used the Triple P principles personally and with thousands of families, and found it to be effective and family friendly.

FAQ

Questions I hear often

What does a session actually cost?

Parent coaching prices are shown on the parent page. Schools and organisations are quoted directly because the right format depends on your audience, setting and aims. Every enquiry gets a clear written proposal before anything is booked.

What is Triple P and why do you use it?

Triple P (the Positive Parenting Program) is one of the most extensively researched parenting approaches in the world, with more than 40 years of evidence behind it. It's used by the NHS, local authorities and schools across the UK. It gives parents practical tools rather than slogans, which is why I've stayed with it for so long.

Do you work online or only in person?

Both. Parent coaching is available UK-wide over video call. School talks, workshops and organisational consultancy are in person, but I'll travel across the UK, and virtual delivery is available on request.

Is this therapy? Do I need a referral?

No, this is coaching and consultancy, not therapy, and no referral is needed. If something comes up in our work that would be better supported by a clinical service (for example CAMHS or a GP), I'll say so clearly and help you find the right route.

How do you handle safeguarding?

I work to a full safeguarding policy shared with every client at the start of our work, hold an up-to-date enhanced DBS, and carry professional insurance. Full documentation is available for schools and organisations on request.
