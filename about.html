My story

From frontline practitioner to independent consultant.

The charity I led for a decade closed in [placeholder: year]. Rather than step away from the work, I took the chance to rebuild it — leaner, more focused, and with the same families at the centre.

My background is unusual. I trained as [placeholder: original discipline — social work / early years / health visiting], spent a decade embedded in community support services, and then led a regional parenting charity working alongside local authorities, schools and the NHS.

What I learned in those years: generic parenting advice rarely survives a Tuesday evening. Real change comes from strategies that families can actually use on the school run, at the dinner table, at bedtime — not in a clinic. That's why I only work with frameworks that have been tested in the real world, primarily the Triple P (Positive Parenting Program), one of the most heavily researched parenting models in the world.

Since going independent, I've been able to focus entirely on the work that makes a difference — 1-to-1 coaching with parents, talks and workshops in schools, and strategic consultancy for organisations that want to do parenting support properly.

How I work

Evidence-based, non-judgemental, practical.

Three principles shape everything I do — with parents in my coaching room, with staff in a school hall, and with boards in an organisation.

Evidence, not opinion

I use Triple P and other peer-reviewed frameworks — because what works at scale also works around your kitchen table. No fads, no guru energy.

Warmth without judgement

Parenting is already hard enough. My job isn't to critique how you're doing it — it's to add tools to what's already working and quietly take pressure off what isn't.

Measurable outcomes

Every engagement — whether a family or an employer — starts with a clear definition of what "better" looks like and how we'll know when we're there.
