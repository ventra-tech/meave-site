For Organisations

Supporting working parents and the organisations that depend on them

I work with employers, nurseries, charities and local authorities, to design and deliver credible, evidence-based parenting support programmes that strengthen staff wellbeing, engagement and retention.

From strategic consultancy to practical delivery partnerships, my focus is on creating support that is realistic, measurable and genuinely useful for working families.

Who I partner with

Different sectors. One consistent approach

Every partnership begins with the same question: what meaningful outcome are we trying to achieve, and how will we measure success?

Employers & HR Teams

Supporting organisations to better engage and retain employees who are also parents through practical, evidence-based workplace support.

  • Lunch-and-learn sessions
  • Working parent workshops
  • Manager coaching and guidance
  • Return-to-work support

Nurseries & Early Years Settings

Helping early years providers strengthen parental engagement, staff confidence and family support through evidence-based practice.

  • Parent workshop programmes
  • Staff CPD and training
  • Early intervention support
  • Ofsted-ready family engagement frameworks

Charities & Voluntary, Community, and Social Enterprise Organisations

  • Theory of change development

  • Funder and impact reporting

Local Authorities

Commissioned parenting support programmes and strategic consultancy to strengthen family support pathways across communities and services.

  • Delivery of Triple P group programmes

  • Early help and family support strategy
  • Outcomes measurement and reporting
For Employers

Supporting working parents is no longer just a wellbeing initiative

It's a retention, engagement and culture issue too. I help organisations provide practical, credible support for employees who are navigating the realities of parenting, caregiving and family life, in ways that align with both staff wellbeing and organisational goals.

  • Manager training around parental transitions, maternity, paternity, return-to-work and family bereavement
  • Lunch-and-learn sessions covering common parenting challenges: anxiety, sleep, behaviour, neurodiversity and adolescence
  • One-to-one parent coaching offered as part of employee wellbeing support
  • Review of parent-facing policies, communication and workplace culture
  • Reporting and evaluation focused on engagement, feedback, uptake and wellbeing outcomes

Engagement models

Three ways to work with me

Advisory day

A focused day on-site or online. Diagnostic review of your current parenting-support offer, with a written summary and prioritised recommendations within one week.

Ideal for: leaders exploring whether to invest here at all.

Retained partnership

A rolling quarterly engagement, typically 2–3 days per month, covering programme delivery, manager coaching and executive reporting.

Ideal for: HR, People or Wellbeing leads with a live remit.

Project engagement

Scoped, time-limited work with a clear deliverable: a commissioned group programme, a policy overhaul, a pathway redesign, a funder-ready evaluation.

Ideal for: charities, local authorities and children's nurseries with a specific mandate.
