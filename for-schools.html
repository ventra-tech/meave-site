Programmes & talks

A clear menu of formats — adapted to your setting.

Each programme below is a starting point. Content, depth and delivery are shaped around your specific phase, catchment and priorities.

Parent engagement

"The Calm Home" — parent workshop series

A three-session series for parents on setting boundaries, managing big feelings, and reducing conflict at home. Delivered in your hall or online.

  • 3 × 90-minute evening sessions (or twilight equivalent)
  • Takeaway handouts for every family
  • Optional Q&A drop-in for harder-to-reach parents

Best for: primary settings wanting to strengthen home-school behaviour consistency.

Staff CPD

Trauma-aware conversations with parents

A practical CPD day for pastoral staff, SENCOs and senior leaders on how to have difficult parent conversations without escalating them.

  • Full INSET day or two twilight sessions
  • Realistic role-play scenarios from your setting
  • Written framework staff can apply the next morning

Best for: schools where parent complaints or escalations are climbing.

Student wellbeing

Year group assembly series

Age-appropriate talks for pupils on emotional regulation, friendship, and asking for help — backed up by teacher resources for follow-up form time.

  • Single assembly or three-assembly arc
  • Form-time discussion prompts
  • Parent companion note to send home

Best for: schools responding to rising anxiety or peer-conflict referrals.

Keynote

Keynote: "Parents aren't the problem."

A 45-minute signature talk for education and early-years conferences on why genuine parental partnership — not another policy — is the fastest route to student outcomes.

  • 45-minute keynote + 15-minute Q&A
  • Slide deck and speaker notes supplied in advance
  • Can be tailored to your conference theme

Best for: headline slots at MAT conferences, LA events, and sector summits.
