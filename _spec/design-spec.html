1. Brand & Tone

Who Meave is

Meave Darroux is a UK-based parenting consultant and certified Triple P (Positive Parenting Program) practitioner with 15+ years of frontline experience. After a decade leading a regional parenting charity, she has gone independent and serves three audiences:

  1. Parents — 1-to-1 coaching for tantrums, transitions, sleep, screens and siblings.
  2. Schools & events — Ofsted-aligned talks, workshops, keynotes, staff CPD.
  3. Organisations — consultancy and commissioned delivery for nurseries, charities, local authorities, NHS trusts, prisons and employers.

Voice & tone

  • Warm, not soft. The reader feels listened to, never lectured.
  • Practical over theoretical. Strategies you can use on the school run, not in a clinic.
  • Evidence over opinion. Triple P framing earns the right to be confident.
  • Plainspoken. Short sentences. No jargon. No guru energy.
  • UK English. "organisation", "behaviour", "programme".

Visual feel

  • Editorial — like a thoughtful Sunday-magazine feature, not a SaaS landing page.
  • Warm cream surfaces (#FAF6F0) with deep forest green primary and terracotta accent.
  • Generous whitespace, large display serif (Fraunces) for headlines, clean sans (Inter) for body.
  • Homepage = cinematic and full-bleed; inner pages = grounded and informational.

3.7 Animation & motion

Scope: All scroll/word-by-word animations are scoped to the homepage only (presence of .hero-cinematic) and are skipped entirely when prefers-reduced-motion: reduce is set. Mobile-nav and FAQ animations run on every page.

1. Word-by-word hero reveal

  • JS splits .hero-cinematic__h1 textContent into <span class="hw"> per word.
  • Each word gets animation-delay: index * 85ms.
  • Animation: @keyframes wordFadeUp — opacity 0→1, translateY 20px→0, 0.55s cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) forwards.
  • aria-label on h1 retains the full plain-text string for screen readers.

2. Scroll reveal (homepage only)

  • JS adds .reveal class to: .card, .audience-card, .stat, .pillar-card, .testimonial, .step.
  • Section headings (.section__head h2) get .reveal.reveal--left.
  • IntersectionObserver: threshold 0.15, rootMargin 0px 0px -60px 0px.
  • On intersect: index in parent calculated, transitionDelay = idx * 100ms, .revealed class added → opacity 1, translate(0,0).
  • Transition: 0.55s ease.

3. Stat count-up

  • Each stat number has data-count + optional data-suffix.
  • Second IntersectionObserver (threshold 0.4) triggers RAF loop.
  • Duration 1400ms, ease-out quad: 1 - (1 - p)^2.
  • Numbers ≥ 1000 formatted with toLocaleString('en-GB'); suffix appended.

4. Sticky CTA fade (mobile, every page)

  • Hidden on desktop (≥ 1024px).
  • IntersectionObserver watches .hero with rootMargin -40px 0px 0px 0px.
  • When hero leaves viewport → .visible class added → opacity 1, translateY 0.
  • Note: the cinematic homepage uses .hero-cinematic, not .hero, so on home this falls back to "always visible" (acceptable, but worth noting if reskinning).

5. Video play-button pulse

  • About page video placeholder — 72px terracotta circle.
  • @keyframes playPulse 2s ease-in-out infinite: scale 1→1.08 + outward shadow ring fade.

6. Hover micro-interactions

  • Buttons: translateY -1px on hover.
  • Cards: translateY -3px + shadow-md.
  • Text-link arrow: translateX 3px.
  • FAQ icon: rotate 45deg.
  • Audience-card arrow: translateX 4px.

    Triple P (the Positive Parenting Program) is one of the most extensively researched parenting approaches in the world, with more than 40 years of evidence behind it. It's used by the NHS, local authorities and schools across the UK. It gives parents practical tools rather than slogans, which is why I've stayed with it for so long.

For Parents page (6 items)

  1. Is this therapy?
    No. Coaching is practical, forward-looking, and focused on parenting strategy — not processing trauma or diagnosing mental health conditions. If you or your child need clinical support, I'll tell you honestly and can help you find the right route.
  2. What ages do you work with?
    I'm trained in Triple P for families with children from birth to 16. Most of my work is with parents of children aged 2–12, but I regularly support families navigating early teens too.
  3. Do both parents need to attend?
    It helps, but it's not required. I work with single parents, separated co-parents, and one partner on behalf of a household. We'll set up whatever genuinely works for your setup.
  4. Is my child neurodivergent — can you help?
    Yes. A significant portion of the families I work with include autistic and ADHD children. My approach adjusts accordingly — same evidence base, different tools.
  5. How soon will we see change?
    Most families notice concrete shifts within 2–3 weeks of applying the first set of strategies. Lasting change — the sort that holds under pressure — usually takes the full six-week arc.
  6. What if we can't afford the full programme?
    Tell me. I hold a small number of reduced-fee places each month for families who need them, and I can point you to low-cost Triple P group programmes run through the NHS and local authorities.
